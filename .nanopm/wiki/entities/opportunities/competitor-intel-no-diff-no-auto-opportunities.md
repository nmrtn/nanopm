## 1. Problem summary
Competitor intel returns the full picture on every competitor on every run, rather than surfacing only what has changed since the last run. And it stops at observation: it doesn't position changes against the founder's own product/business model, nor auto-promote the gaps it finds into the opportunity database. The founder wants change-only intel that feeds the discovery loop, not a recurring full report he has to re-read and translate himself.

## 2. Value to the user
### Job to be done
The founder wants each intel run to tell him *what's new* — the diff since last time — read against his own product and business model, with any gap it reveals proposed as a new opportunity he can accept into the DB. His alternative today is re-reading a full per-competitor report each run and manually deciding what changed and whether it implies an opportunity.

### Where we fall short
**No change-only (diff) view**
Every run re-presents all information on all competitors instead of just the delta since the last snapshot.
- "Je ne veux pas avoir à chaque fois toutes les informations sur tous les compétiteurs — je veux voir ce qui a changé depuis le dernier run." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)

**No auto-promotion of gaps into the opportunity DB**
Intel observes but doesn't close the loop: it could propose creating opportunities from the gaps it finds, and doesn't.
- "Il y a peut-être un truc intéressant à ce que compétiteur Intel propose de créer des opportunités automatiquement." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)

## 3. Value to the company
Closes the intel→discovery loop: change-only intel positioned against the company's own product/business model, with gaps auto-suggested as opportunities, makes competitor monitoring an input to the opportunity database rather than a standalone report. Reinforces the "stays current / partner not generator" theme that runs through this interview.
