## Strategic implications

1. **Linear's "Shared Skills for Linear Agent" is the most consequential signal.** Linear has the exact phrase nanopm uses ("recurring tasks like specs, planning, and updates") inside the surface nanopm hands off to. The form-factor bet (macOS viewer) was framed against the terminal — it now also has to be framed against "Linear already has skills." Recommend recruiting at least one prototype cohort participant whose default surface is Linear (not VSCode/terminal) and explicitly testing whether they prefer a separate viewer or Linear's native skills surface. This is the kind of counterfactual the strategy's "terminal-comfortable control" is designed to expose, but Linear-native users are a distinct third arm.

2. **ChatPRD is the existence proof and the cautionary tale.** 100k+ PMs and a Teams plan with Linear integration baked in says the "AI-as-PM" market is real and is already monetized at $29/seat. nanopm's differentiation against ChatPRD is the **multi-source signal aggregation** (App Store / Reddit / interviews / support / competitors — this skill itself) and the **typed/falsifiable state layer**. ChatPRD does AI Coaching ("CPO-level reviews with strategic gap analysis") — adjacent to nanopm's adversarial gates. Worth a deep look at what their "AI Coaching" actually produces; if it's similar to `/pm-strategy`'s adversarial challenge, the moat is the schema enforcement, not the challenge itself.

3. **Cursor's Skills + team marketplace makes "skill pack distribution" a commodity.** "MCPs, skills, and hooks" is now an Individual feature and "team marketplace for internal rules, skills, and plugins" is a Teams feature. nanopm's distribution-as-skills model can be reproduced inside Cursor by any sufficiently motivated team. Reinforces STRATEGY.md's point: the moat is the *synthesis across sources* plus the *typed/falsifiable state*, not the skill packaging.

4. **The handoff direction matters.** ChatPRD is listed as a Linear Agent. Cursor is listed as a Linear Agent. nanopm hands off TO Linear via `/pm-breakdown`. If the agent surface inside Linear is where PMs actually live, the form-factor pivot's macOS viewer is moving *away* from that surface, not toward it. Either (a) the macOS viewer is the right wedge for the non-Linear-native segment (true solo founders pre-tool), or (b) the eventual play is also a "nanopm Linear Agent." STRATEGY.md should probably note the Linear-agent path as a contingency if the prototype cohort reads positive but the value-add is "easier access to artifacts" rather than "I don't want to be in Linear."
