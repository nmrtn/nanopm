## 1. Problem summary
Turning discovery from on-demand into a daily continuous sweep multiplies the volume of raw signal — competitor diffs, feedback items, analytics movements, scraped community threads. For a solo builder with no time to triage, high volume without synthesis becomes noise rather than insight: more to read, not more to act on. The value of continuous discovery is lost unless the firehose is distilled into a small number of things that actually matter.

## 2. Value to the user
### Job to be done
When discovery is running every day, I want to open it and immediately see "here is what changed and what it means for me," so that I can decide what to do in minutes — not wade through a daily dump of raw items and do the synthesis myself. The alternative today is reading everything manually and trying to spot what's significant, which doesn't scale once the cadence is daily.
### Where we fall short
**Raw signal, not synthesized insight**
Continuous runs produce volume; without a synthesis layer the PM gets a pile of items rather than a ranked, interpreted "what's new and why it matters."

**No filtering of significance**
Routine, low-importance changes are surfaced alongside genuinely consequential ones, so the PM has to re-triage every day instead of being pointed at the few signals worth acting on.

## 3. Value to the company
If continuous discovery (the autonomy bet's Phase 2) produces noise, PMs will mute or ignore it — defeating the retention case for an always-on loop. Synthesis is what converts continuous discovery from a cost (more to read) into the daily habit that keeps users in the product.
