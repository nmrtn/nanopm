# PRD: 5-Person Behavioral Demand Test
Generated by /pm-prd on 2026-04-07
Project: nanopm
Status: DRAFT

---

## Problem Statement

The strategy's core assumption isn't about where people plan — it's whether latent demand for structured PM process exists in the segment at all. A solo developer who doesn't do structured planning doesn't want a structured planning tool in any channel. The location poll tests the distribution assumption. This test goes one level deeper: does nanopm actually change what a person does or decides, in a way they notice and articulate within 48 hours of using it?

This is the cheapest possible test of the demand assumption before committing Q2 capacity to distribution. If zero of five people say the artifact changed a decision, the demand assumption is wrong and distribution is irrelevant.

---

## User Stories

- As the nanopm author, I need behavioral signal (not just install count) from 5 solo developers so I can confirm latent demand for structured PM process before distributing to a wider audience.
- As a solo developer asked to try nanopm, I want a clear, minimal ask (run this, answer one question 48h later) so that I can participate without feeling like I'm doing unpaid QA work.

---

## Success Criteria

| Criteria | How Measured | Target |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Participation | Number of solo devs who complete `/pm-run` on their actual project | 5 completions (if fewer than 5 complete it, note where they dropped out — that's also signal) |
| Behavioral change | Count who answer "yes" to "Did this change anything you did or decided?" | ≥1 of 5 says yes → demand assumption survives; 0/5 → assumption is wrong |
| Friction observation | Where do people get stuck, give up, or ask questions? | At least 1 friction point identified per participant — this is the first-stranger observation deliverable |
| What will be different in commits after this ships? | Review CONTEXT.md and STRATEGY.md 7 days post-test | CONTEXT.md Q10 or Q8 updated with behavioral test result. STRATEGY.md "The Risk" section updated with outcome. One of: (a) "2026-04-14: 5-person test complete, X/5 confirmed behavior change — demand assumption survives" OR (b) "2026-04-14: 5-person test complete, 0/5 behavior change — demand reframing needed." |

**Pass threshold:** ≥1 of 5 says yes. This is deliberately low — if latent demand exists, at least one person in five should notice a decision changed. Zero is a strong signal that the product isn't delivering on its core claim.

---

## Scope

### In scope (v1)
- Recruit 5 solo developers. Criteria: writing code AND making product decisions on the same project, using Claude Code (or willing to install it), not in the builder's immediate circle.
- Ask each to run `/pm-run` on their actual current project — not a toy project, not a demo. Real project only.
- 48 hours after their run: send one question via DM or reply: "Did the output from nanopm change anything you did or decided — even one small thing?"
- Record each response (yes/no + what changed, or why not).
- Update CONTEXT.md and STRATEGY.md with result within 24 hours of last response.

### Out of scope (v1)
- Structured interview or follow-up research session — too much friction, defeats the purpose of "cheapest test"
- More than 5 participants — this is signal gathering, not statistical research. 5 is enough to confirm or deny.
- Fixing bugs found during the test before all 5 complete — observe first, fix after. Fixing during contaminates the observation.
- Screensharing or live observation sessions — async only. Real use = unsupervised use.

---

## Requirements

### Recruitment requirements
1. At least 3 of 5 must be people the builder has never had a product/code conversation with (strangers or near-strangers). Friends who haven't used nanopm are acceptable for 2 of 5.
2. All 5 must have a real ongoing project — not a demo app, not a side project they haven't touched in 3 months.
3. Recruitment message must not oversell: "I built a PM planning tool for Claude Code. Would you be willing to run it on your current project and tell me one thing about your experience 48h later?" No pitch, no feature list.

### Execution requirements
1. Give each participant the GitHub URL only — no setup help, no walkthrough. Observe what they figure out vs. what breaks.
2. Log friction points in real-time as they come in (DM questions, GitHub issues, replies). Each friction point is a future fix.
3. Send the 48h follow-up question on schedule — don't skip it if you don't hear from them. Silence is also data.
4. Do not interpret results while the test is running. Write down the pass/fail decision rule now (≥1 of 5 says yes) and apply it after all 5 respond.

### Documentation requirements
- Keep a running log: participant handle (or initials), project type, completion status, friction points observed, 48h response.
- Do not publish this log publicly — it's internal validation data.
