## How It Makes Money

**Free open-source skill pack — no monetization today, deliberately deferred.**

nanopm is distributed as an MIT-licensed pack of markdown skills + bash runtime + Python validators + a SwiftUI viewer prototype, installed via `curl | bash`. Users pay nothing. No revenue model has been chosen. By explicit founder call (reconfirmed this run), monetization is not a Q3 2026 priority: the quarter's job is to prove a real founder voluntarily returns to a full pipeline within 21 days ([VISION-MISSION.md](VISION-MISSION.md) "the One Belief", [STRATEGY.md](STRATEGY.md) proof quarter), not to charge.

This deferral sits inside an explicitly *offensive* mission — automating the PM loop for AI-agent-native founders ([VISION-MISSION.md](VISION-MISSION.md)) — and that ambition does not soften the OSS stance today. It does sharpen the trigger for ending it: see "Riskiest Assumption" below.

## Revenue Streams

- **None today.** Zero revenue, by design. Distribution = free `setup` script + GitHub clone.

The doc names this plainly rather than inventing a stream. A future stream (paid macOS viewer, hosted SaaS, services, sponsorship) may emerge once retention is proven, but none of those are committed today and any would require a separate strategy run.

## Pricing & Packaging

| Tier / Package | Price | Who it's for | What unlocks it |
|----------------|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| nanopm OSS (skill pack + viewer prototype, one MIT bundle) | $0 — MIT licence | Anyone running Claude Code / Mistral Vibe / OpenAI Codex (primary: Terminal-native Theo; secondary: Designer-founder Dani on the viewer surface) | Nothing gated; full surface available on install |

There is no second tier. The CLI skill pack and the macOS viewer prototype ship under the same MIT licence as a single product, even though [PRODUCT.md](PRODUCT.md) maps them as two surfaces. No "Pro" features, no per-seat metering, no enterprise SKU.

**Packaging tensions to flag:**
- **Two surfaces, one bundle.** The product now has two surfaces (CLI skill pack + viewer, [PRODUCT.md](PRODUCT.md)) and two personas living on different sides (Terminal-native Theo on CLI, Designer-founder Dani on viewer, [PERSONAS.md](PERSONAS.md)). If a paid surface ever exists, it almost certainly lives on the viewer side — but the viewer is explicitly throwaway today, so any packaging decision is premature until the proof tests in [STRATEGY.md](STRATEGY.md) read.
- **Anti-persona guardrail.** [PERSONAS.md](PERSONAS.md) explicitly names non-builders evaluating nanopm as standalone PM SaaS as the anti-persona — serving them means rebuilding as PM SaaS (hosted editor, our LLM calls, our auth), which competes with Linear on price and kills the agent-native value prop. Any future packaging move must respect this guardrail: the model is "PM-OS inside an agent," not "PM SaaS that runs nanopm under the hood."

## The Riskiest Assumption

**That monetization can be deferred indefinitely without the deferral itself becoming the failure mode** — i.e. that "we'll figure out the model after retention proves" is a safe sequence rather than a way to keep building a free tool that never crosses the line into a business. The corollary risk: whatever revenue model eventually fits nanopm (paid viewer, hosted SaaS, services, sponsorship) may require an audience, a packaging, or a code structure that the OSS-only era is actively shaping *against*.

The behavior-gap signal from [CHALLENGES.md](CHALLENGES.md) sharpens the risk: 4 viewer-polish commits and 0 recruitment messages between recent audits suggests the deferral could compound into "the trigger never fires because the cohort is never recruited." The trigger below is only meaningful if recruitment actually happens by the [AUDIT.md](AUDIT.md) 2026-06-14 deadline.

**Falsifiable trigger (unchanged):** at the end of Q3 2026, if either proof cohort in [STRATEGY.md](STRATEGY.md) reads PASS, name a revenue model within 30 days. If neither reads PASS, the deferral is moot — there's nothing to monetize anyway.
