## 1. Problem summary
nanopm's capture→process bricks work (opportunities, solutions, PRDs), but the brick that turns them into a prioritized, labeled, continuously-updated plan — the orchestration of opportunities + solutions + objectives into something concrete to work on next — does not exist for the one user who completes pipelines. He has actively abandoned `/pm-roadmap`. He names this orchestration as **the core of nanopm's value**, not a nice-to-have: it's the original promise ("I'll help you know what to work on in priority") restated back at the product as its weakest point. Three sub-gaps: wrong granularity / no connection to solutions; no problem/opportunity labels on roadmap items; no multiple altitudes (an execution roadmap for the team vs a stakeholder-level roadmap); and the plan isn't kept living (challenged, updated, explained almost daily).

## 2. Value to the user
### Job to be done
The founder wants nanopm to *orchestrate* everything upstream into a single living roadmap he can act on: a prioritized list of solutions, each tagged with the problem/opportunity it answers, kept fresh and explained as it changes — and viewable at more than one altitude. His alternative today is the Notion roadmap he used at Photoroom (a list of solutions mapped to labels showing which problem/opportunity each answers) — exactly what nanopm doesn't give him, which is why he abandoned the skill and falls back.

### Where we fall short
**Roadmap is the heart of the value but the orchestration brick is missing**
The upstream bricks are good; the layer that mobilizes them into a daily-challenged, daily-updated, explained plan doesn't exist.
- "Je pense que roadmap, c'est le cœur de la valeur de ce que tu peux m'offrir, c'est comment tu orchestres toutes les briques que tu as déjà en place qui sont bien. Mais comment tu les mets en œuvre pour que derrière la roadmap, [presque] tous les jours, elle est challengée, updatée, je comprends pourquoi tu as fait des choix, je comprends ce qui a été modifié." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)
- "Cette coupe-là [opportunité → solution → roadmap] on ne close pas bien... il faut que ce soit hyper smooth, stratégie, objectif, opportunités très claires, et derrière la liste des solutions et la magie fait que tu sors ta roadmap qui est hyper claire au niveau que tu souhaites." — Granola call w/ Nico, 2026-06-30 (raw/interviews/2972bcf0bb4c.md)

**Wrong granularity, no connection to solutions**
The roadmap's contents aren't at the right level and aren't linked to the solutions database.
- "À les contenus que j'ai vus dans la roadmap, je ne les trouvais pas au bon niveau de granularité, il n'y a pas de connexion avec les solutions." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)

**No problem/opportunity labels on roadmap items**
He wants the roadmap to be a list of solutions, each with a small label showing which problem and which opportunity it answers — the Notion/Photoroom pattern — and it's absent.
- "Cette logique de priorisation dans la roadmap qui devrait quand même être quelque chose de très important, j'ai du mal aujourd'hui à le voir... je voudrais que ma roadmap soit une liste de solutions mappées avec des petits labels qui me montrent quel problème ça répond, à quelle opportunity. Et ça je ne l'ai pas — c'est quelque chose que j'utilisais beaucoup dans Notion chez Photoroom." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)

**Not proactive — doesn't keep the plan aligned on its own**
He abandoned `/pm-roadmap` partly because it isn't the proactive thing that looks at the opportunities, the solutions, and the current objectives and ensures alignment.
- "[pm-roadmap] je l'ai un peu abandonné... on n'est pas dans ce truc qui est proactif et qui me pose des questions, qui regarde la liste des opportunités, la liste des solutions, les objectifs en cours, s'assurer qu'il y a l'alignement." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)

**No multiple altitudes (execution vs stakeholder)**
A single roadmap can't serve both the team's execution view and the stakeholder view; he wants at least two.
- "J'aimerais bien presque avoir plusieurs niveaux de roadmap : une roadmap d'exécution avec mes équipes, avec mes listes de solutions, [et] une roadmap qui est un peu plus au niveau de mes stakeholders." — self-interview, 2026-06-29 (Granola dc61e5c2)

## 3. Value to the company
This is the founder's own stated heart-of-value and the single largest gap named by the only user who completes pipelines — yet the current roadmap NOW cycle spends ~all capacity on wiki-substrate depth and zero on this prioritization loop, a direct contradiction to bring into the planning conversation. It is also the orchestration layer that the retention bet leans on: the reason to come back is a plan that stays current and tells you what's next. Guardrail (the interview's own framing): this is N=1, the friendliest possible user — strong product signal, but not a substitute for the deferred external-Theo recruitment.
